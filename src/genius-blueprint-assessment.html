About the Genius Blueprint Profiles

There are a total of eight Genius Blueprint profiles and each one plays an equally important role in a team. In any business, there is a natural progression to follow to successfully complete a project, similar to the natural growth cycles that occurs in nature.

In nature, the cycle of creation starts in spring with a seed that shoots up through the soil. In summer, this seed will grow into a plant with the support of people who care for it. In autumn, the plant produces fruit that will be collected and distributed. When winter arrives, the plant will hibernate and slow down in preparation for spring.

The Genius Blueprint categorizes the different profiles by seasons, because business follows a similar growth cycle. Spring represents the ideation phase, because this is when people ask themselves what it is that they want to create. This is also the time when the vision for an idea takes shape and becomes a prototype. Summer represents the expansion and amplification phase. This is where the majority of growth happens. During this phase, people will take the prototype and develop it into something can be replicated and applied. The implementation phase occurs in the Fall. This is when planning and organization takes place and the product or service is tested to ensure that it delivers what was intended. Winter is the the analysis and reproduction phase. During this time, people will review what is working and will simplify and automate the process.

HOW THE GENIUS BLUEPRINT ASSESSMENT ALSO BENEFITS THE ORGANIZATION

It is also possible to activate superpowers and upgrade mindset on an organizational level. Research has found that the conditions for creating a high performing team include psychological safety, dependability, structure and clarity, meaning, and impact.

The Genius Blueprint helps create these conditions within an organization, by changing the organization’s mindset, or level of consciousness.

Organizations can be viewed as superhuman living systems similar to biological systems, where a single cell represents the individual, a group of cells represents a team, and the biological system represents the organization. Each group of cells will have a different function similar to how organs have different functions in the body. The organs work together to help the body run optimally. What makes the organization superhuman and different from a biological system is that it has its own way of thinking, values, and beliefs. This creates the organization’s culture and drives its behaviours.

An organization’s mindset or level of consciousness determines how they see their people, who they hire, how they work, and what kind of product they create. It also influences how they see their customers, partners, and employees. Every organization has a Genius potential waiting to be activated and realized. As an organization upgrades its mindset and accesses its Genius, its adaptability and potential for unique and innovative products increases.
